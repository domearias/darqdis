01 · Why me

The strongest brands are built on trust, in every single detail.

I'm a spatial and interior designer with 6+ years of experience translating brand identity into physical environments.

My work spans retail, hospitality, and residential, from concept development and spatial storytelling through materials specification, lighting design, 3D visualisation, and full construction documentation. I own projects end-to-end, from idea through execution and delivery.

Currently designing luxury interiors at LUV Studio in Barcelona, I also lead darqdis, an architecture education platform with 50K+ followers where I teach spatial design and AI-assisted workflows to Spanish-speaking architects.

I believe the most powerful brand experiences happen when digital-first companies invest in physical spaces that feel as intentional, elevated, and immersive as their products. That's what I want to help you build.

02

Sisters Kitchen

Soul-warming comfort food.

A full-scope retail spatial design project that translated a brand born in a pandemic dark kitchen into an immersive hospitality environment. Starting from concept development, I designed the complete spatial customer journey, from storefront threshold to dining experience, delivering a 90-page design manual covering brand analysis, spatial planning, custom furniture design (fluted bar with gold detailing, organic micro-cement patio bench), materials specification, lighting design, MEP coordination, and construction documentation.

The design connects guests to the brand's core identity: comfort food that hugs the soul. Natural materials and organic forms create a warm environment, while gold accents add sophistication to the earthy palette. Every spatial decision reinforces the brand's philosophy of honest, intentional food.

03

Piza

Author sourdough pizzas in a fast-casual setting.

An artisan pizzeria where the spatial customer journey begins with the ritual of the open oven, the heart of the space, visible from every angle. The design translates the brand's craft-first identity into a physical environment through raw industrial materials softened with warm lighting design and natural textures. Every spatial decision supports the brand narrative: watching dough become pizza is the experience framework that organises the entire floor plan.

Holy Cow

The creative ice creams.

An ice cream concept store where the product IS the spatial palette. The design translates Holy Cow's playful brand identity into a physical environment where colour, texture, and materiality mirror the ingredient-driven menu. Customers described it as “the most creative and curious spot in all of Quito.” It's proof that brand-to-environment translation creates memorable, immersive experiences that customers talk about unprompted.
